About Us

FIRST Team 6513 - Wyvern Robotics is based out of Woodbridge College in Woodbridge, Ontario. We are the second FIRST Robotics team in the York Region District School Board and one of thousands of teams around the world.

This is our second year competing in the FIRST Robotics Competition and this year we plan to push ourselves further in our pursuit of encouraging STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Math) education in the community, getting more students involoved in our school, and building the best robot we possibly can!

Wyvern Robotics is composed of students, teachers, and community mentors from many fields; all working together to build amazing robots. Students work together to solve problems and learn to use the knowledge gained in the classroom and apply it to a real-world project. This program sets students apart from the rest of the pack when preparing for post-secondary and the job market. In a way, the robots build the students.

Our Mission

FIRST Team 6513's mission is to educate students in the realms of STEAM.

We feel as though we've been given such an amazing apportunity to have teachers and community mentors who are supportive of our interest in STEAM and have given us much of their time and knowledge. We feel that we should take all of the things that we have been fortunate to learn and give younger students the same experience.

Under our Building Blocks Initiative (BBI), we plan to host Hours of Code in the communiyt, conduct presentaitons, and demonstrate how much fun learning about technology can be!

Robots

2017-2018 FIRST Robot

Spyro

Spyro competed in the 2017-18 FIRST Robotics Competition challenge - FIRST PowerUp!


Drivetrain

  • Six wheel tank drive (rear and center powered, front idle)
  • Rear and center wheels are 5" Colson wheels with custom hex hubs, front are 4" Vex Omni for maneuverability
  • Powered by 4 CIMs (2 each side)
  • Custom riveted Vex Versa Chassis with gearbox integrated into rails
  • C-channel exterior rails make for easy bumper application and removal
  • Solid 1" aluminum bumper mounts add to the structural rigidity of the robot for climbing with 3 robots and do not add to the weight of the robot since the bumoers are weighed separately

Intake

  • Built for scoring in the switches and exchange
  • 4 green AndyMark compliant wheels counter-rotate to grab the Power Cubes from any direction
  • Powered by BAG Motors with a 7:1 VersaPlanetary Lite Reduction
  • Arm lifted by a tow strap powered by 49:1 VersaPlanetary and 775Pro motor, lowered by gravity
  • Pneumatic cylinders with capped ends provide a linear "air spring" for the jaws to be able to freely open and accept the Power Cubes

Tower

  • Comprised of 2"x1" structural tube and 2"x1" C-channel, skateboard bearings
  • Independantly driven up and down for speed lift and power collapse. Speed lift - lifts the tower to grab onto the rungs, power-collapse - collapses the tower with very high torque to be able to lift 3 robots
  • Speed lift powered by a Mini CIM and 10:1 VersaPlanetary
  • PowerCollapse powered by 2 CIMs, 5:1 VersaPlanetary Gearbox, and 4:1 afterbox spur gear reduction
  • Tower extends through the middle of the rungs and fires 2 small pneumatic cylinders outward to latch onto the rungs

Wings

  • Once the tower is locked onto the rungs and the time reaches 30 seconds remaining, 2 pneumatic cylinders retract to release the wings
  • Wings fold down to be parallel to the floor
  • Stoppers on the chassis stop the wings from going too far
  • Wings are large enough for all sizes of robots drivetrains to drive on without issue
  • Platforms are intentionally cut away from the base of the robot to lock in the wheels of alliance robots
  • hinged ramps help alliance robots to climb the platforms

Control

  • 4 autonomous modes selected during DisabledPeriodic (Drive Straight, Cube - Start Left, Cube - Start Right)
  • Encoders inform the robot of distance travelled
  • String pot gives position of intake arm and can is used for preset angles (Intake(Floor), Travel(Up), Shoot(Angled))
  • Manual mode for intake arm also available by moving the axis
  • Robot is controlled by 2 controlles. One for the drivebase and one for all auxiliary features
